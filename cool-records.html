Cool Records

Here's a list of some interesting records that still exist today:

  • Longest match ever: 11 hours and five minutes between John Isner and Nicolas Mahut, ending with a score of 6-4, 3-6, 6-7, 7-6, 70-68.
  • Most grand slam titles: Roger Federer with 20.
  • Most consecutive weeks as world number 1: Roger Federer at 237
  • Most aces in a season: Goran Ivanišević who had 1,477 in 1996.
  • Rackets smashed in a season: Marat Safin who broke 48 rackets in 1999.
  • ATP singles titles: Jimmy Connors with 109 singles titles.
  • Most double faults in a match: Marc Rosset had 26 double faults against Michael Joyce in a 1995 Wimbledon game. A double fault is when a server hits two his serve into the net or out two times in a row, giving his opponent a point.
  • Youngest player in top 25: Aaron Krickstein when he was 16 years old in 1983.
  • Consecutive grand slam appearances: Wayne Ferreira appeared at a record 56 consecutive grand slams.
