Top Players of the Open Era

  1. Roger Federer

    2. Most Grand Slam titles at 20. Widely considered the greatest tennis player of all time and has yet to retire at the age of 37. High versatile style of play, as he is effective as both a base-liner and volleyer. Shown above left.

  2. Novak Djokovic

    3. Has 13 Grand Slam titles with many years left to play. Dominant at baseline but can break away from that style of play when necessary. May become greatest player in history. Shown above right.

  3. Pete Sampras

    4. Career featured big rivalry with Andre Agassi. He holds 14 Grand Slam titles, tying him with Nadal for second most. One of best all-around players in history of sport. He could beat players in a variety of ways on a variety of surfaces.

  4. Rod Laver

    5. 11 Grand Slam titles. Former world number 1. Very good on all court surfaces of his time: grass, clay, hard, carpet, and wood. Contributed to five Davis Cup titles for Australia during an age when the Davis Cup was viewed as significant as the four majors.

  5. Rafael Nadal

    6. Has won 16 Grand Slam titles and is still playing. Often called “The King of Clay” due to his 10 Grand Slam titles at the French Open, played on clay court. Tied for second in most Grand Slam titles.

  6. Ivan Lendl

    7. Former world number 1. 8 Grand Slam singles titles. His game included intense topspin and strength to dominate opponents, introducing “power tennis” to the sport.

  7. Bjorn Borg

    8. 11 Grand Slam titles. Former world number 1. Very successful in early career, but stardom forced him to retire at the early age of 26. May have broken many records if he had continued playing.

  8. Jimmy Connors

    9. Most tournament singles titles in ATP history at 109 including 8 Grand Slam titles. Also has played the most matches and holds record for most match wins in Open Era.

  9. John McEnroe

    10. 7 Grand Slam singles title. Has very good net play and can mix up his playing style enough to confuse his opponents. Also known for his outbursts on the court, making him one of the most iconic tennis players.

  10. Andre Agassi

    11. 8 Grand Slam singles titles and gold medal at the 1996 Olympic Games. Strong from deep in the court. Has one of best all-time win rates in Open Era tennis.
